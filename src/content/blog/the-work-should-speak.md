This site is deliberately quiet. There is no animated hero, no wall of logos, no
list of twelve services. That restraint is not modesty. It is the same instinct
I bring to software: say less, and make what remains legible.

## Quiet is a decision, not an absence

It is easy to mistake a busy interface for a serious one. More sections, more
claims, more motion. But every element you add competes with the ones that
matter. A landing page that tries to say everything ends up saying nothing in
particular.

So the rule here is simple. A page earns each thing it shows:

- The work comes first, because the work is the argument.
- The writing comes second, because it shows how I think.
- Everything else gets out of the way.

> The goal was never to look impressive. It was to be understood quickly by the
> right person, and ignored painlessly by everyone else.

## The same instinct, in code

A system that has grown hard to reason about rarely needs more features. It
needs fewer load-bearing ideas, named clearly, with the incidental complexity
pared back until the important parts are obvious again.

That is most of the job. The website just practices what the work preaches.
